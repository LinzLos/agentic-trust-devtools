Designer's cut · the opener
Decision
Open with the pattern, not the product. Three versions of the same move, so you watch the skip button degrade: you could decline, you couldn't skip, you weren't even there.
Why
If I open on a coding agent, it reads as a niche dev-tools fix. Show the checkout, the phone call, then the agent, and it's obvious this is happening on every surface. The problem is everywhere; dev tools is just where I can show it cleanly.
Trade-off
Low-fi on purpose. A real screenshot and a plain transcript read in five seconds. Polished mockups would slow the trend down and add nothing.

Designer's cut · the hero
Decision
This beat is the fix. One standing rule, written once, and the agent ranks by it. Every control shows its effect on the spot.
Why
The problem already landed in beat 1, so I don't restage it. Pay-to-rank carries the whole before/after in one view: flip it on and the paid option buys its way to #1, flip it off and the best fit wins.
Trade-off
The sponsored option isn't hidden and isn't banned. It's ranked where it actually fits, shown with its real deal, and routed to your queue. That's the bet: a market that ranks by fit beats one that blocks ads, because good products still reach you and paid placement just can't jump the line.

Designer's cut · the audit
Decision
The queue is a ledger, not a setting. A log you can read, with who each offer served and why it was held on every row.
Why
"Held, not blocked" only works if the held thing is visible and easy to get back. And the same queue is your shopping surface: you open it when you're ready to buy, and what's waiting is matched to fit.
Trade-off
A queue is one more place to maintain. Worth it. It turns "the agent screened your ads" into something you can check in three seconds, and it's where buying actually happens, on your clock instead of mid-task.
the pattern → the fix

What I'm describing doesn't exist today.
But every precondition has shipped.

Ads don't die when you remove them. They climb a layer, into the agent's choices. The fix isn't a “Sponsored” label. It's allegiance: the agent works for you. It screens the injected offer and reroutes it to your terms. You declare the rule once, and the agent holds the line.

That's the horizontal pattern. Dev tools is where I showed it.
